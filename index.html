Traveling in Russia

The real country is not in the news broadcasts, but here.

What haven't we seen there?

According to surveys, 95% of Russians dream of going somewhere, but only 36% plan to spend their vacation in their own country. They say, "What haven't we seen here, at home? In reality, Russia is a whole universe with the gentle sea of the south, dense forests of Sayan, and severe ice of the Putorana Plateau. We have selected and described some interesting places worthy of a vacation.

  • The Curonian Spit

    URL

    park-kosa.ru

    Here, amidst forests and sandy dunes, you can witness two water horizons — the calm waters of the Curonian Lagoon on one side and the Baltic Sea with its rippling waves on the other. It is a unique natural area located on the edge of the Russian enclave.

    The Kaliningrad Oblast doesn't end there. For travelers and explorers, in close proximity, there is the westernmost point of Russia, the Baltic Spit, as well as the German heritage scattered across small coastal towns. The atmosphere of these places is free from hustle and bustle, immersing visitors in the tranquility of nature and the scent of the cool, steel-like sea.

  • The Kola Peninsula

    URL

    National Geographic

    Almost the entire peninsula is located beyond the Arctic Circle. To the south of it lies the Sámi tundra, transitioning to taiga, while to the north is the Arctic Ocean, masquerading as the Barents Sea.

    Your dream of witnessing the Northern Lights begins to come true with a ticket to Murmansk.

  • Altai

    URL

    Facebook

    Altai — one of the most beautiful places in Russia. First and foremost, because of the mountains: if you travel along the ridge, you will see slopes covered with pine trees, mountain rivers, and lakes. And if you open the windows in your car, you can experience the invisible wonder of this place — the mountain air.

    The climate in Altai is moderate, so it is best to visit here in the summer. This way, you will see the full diversity of the local flora and fauna. Moose roam through the forests of Altai, eagles soar above the ridges, and deer graze on the plains. And the famous Pallas's cats are also inhabitants of the Altai region.

  • Winter Baikal

    URL

    https://vk.com/baikalmile

    Baikal is widely known as the largest lake in the world. Many also know that it is the largest source of fresh water and one of the most beautiful places in Russia.

    Indeed, that's true. Baikal is also an ideal place for skijoring competitions. Skijoring is a sport where a skier is attached to a motorcycle, and the tandem tries to achieve the highest possible speed on the ice. In March 2019, during the 'Baikal Mile' festival, a world record was set at 197.011 km/h.

  • Karelia

    URL

    http://vodlozero.ru/

    Siberia doesn't end at the Ural Mountains; it extends into Karelia. However, the Siberian larch, which forms the taiga, does not grow west of Lake Vodlozero. Instead, it reaches heights of 30 meters here. The forests of the Karelian national parks have never known the axe due to impassable swamps. Some pines are over half a millennium old. Touch a living being that witnessed the sun long before Ivan the Terrible did. In the pristine forest, you won't find a trail for hundreds of kilometers. And on rare paths, trees a couple of meters above the ground are marked with bear claws, so everyone knows who the master is here.
